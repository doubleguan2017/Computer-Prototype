Broken Leg Symptons

The major symptoms of a broken leg are pain, swelling, and deformity.
A broken leg can be very obvious, but may require an X-ray to diagnose.
Even when very obvious, an X-ray usually is required to evaluate
the severity of the fracture and exact alignment of bone fragments.

Broken Leg Treatment

A broken bone in the leg requires medical treatment.
Home-care consists of keeping the leg with the broken bone immobilized,
elevated and wrapped in an ice pack until medical help can be sought.

Broken Leg Self-Care at Home

If an injury occurs and a fracture is suspected, remember the following:
1.Immobilize the leg as much as possible until help arrives.
2.Rest. Try to keep from aggravating the injury.
3.Apply an ice pack wrapped in a pillowcase or towel to decrease swelling.
4.If possible, keep the leg elevated with pillows or cushions to decrease swelling.
5.Often with a broken leg, an operation is necessary. Do not let someone with a broken leg eat or drink anything until seen by the doctor.

Broken Leg Prevention

To decrease the risk of injury from a car accident, use a seat belt.
For children, use a safety seat appropriate for the child's age and weight.
If participating in sports in which high speeds or heights are present,
participate in the activity at your experience level and make use of
proper protective gear. If a person is at risk for falling or has an unsteady walk,
use a walker or cane for assistance.
Talk to a doctor about screening for diseases that may weaken bones.
